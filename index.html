Scientific Overview

Sexual reproduction involves the fusion of male gametes (sperm) with female gametes (eggs) to form a zygote, which develops into an embryo and then a fetus.

Human Fertilization Process

Millions of sperm begin their journey through the reproductive tract, but only one will successfully fertilize the egg.

Similarities & Differences

Aspect Humans Plants
Gamete Production Meiosis produces sperm & eggs Meiosis produces pollen & ovules
Fertilization Internal fertilization Fertilization in ovule
Development Embryo develops in womb Embryo develops in seed
Reproduction Types Sexual only Sexual & Asexual

Advantages of Sexual Reproduction

  • Genetic variation increases survival
  • Adaptation to environmental changes
  • Elimination of harmful mutations

Disadvantages

  • Requires two parents
  • Energy-intensive process
  • Slower reproduction rate

Asexual Reproduction in Plants

Runners

Runners

Horizontal stems that grow along the ground and produce new plants at nodes. Common in strawberries.

Fast colonization Low genetic diversity

Tubers

Tubers

Underground storage organs that can sprout new plants. Potatoes are a common example.

Stores energy Limited dispersal

Cuttings

Cuttings

Plant parts that can grow into new individuals when placed in suitable conditions. Used in horticulture.

Preserves traits Vulnerable to disease
